Court Manager

A quick guide to getting started.

Getting Started

When you first open Court Manager, you'll see three options:

  • ⚡ Quick Play — Jump straight into a new offline Open Play session on this device. No sign-in, no setup — just tell it how many players and their names.
  • Sign in with Google — Unlocks full features: create and manage private sessions, save your preferences across devices, and keep a personal match history.
  • Continue without signing in — Join a public or private session as a guest. You can join and play but can't create sessions, and your info won't be saved for next time.

You can also tap How it Works at the bottom of the entry screen for a short in-app overview at any time.

Quick Play

Tap Quick Play, enter how many players are starting, then enter each player's name (skill level is skipped for speed). Tap Start Playing and you're straight into an Open Play session — entirely offline and local to this device. Nobody else can join it, there's no Settings tab, and nothing is shared online.

Joining a Session (online)

Whether you sign in with Google or continue as a guest, you'll land on the Join a Session screen with three panels:

  • Offline Session — Same idea as Quick Play, but you choose the format first (Open Play, Round Robin, or Tournament) before entering players.
  • Private Session — Pick a session you've previously joined, or create a new one (sign-in required to create).
  • Public Session — Pick a location from the dropdown and tap Join.

When you join or create an online session for the first time, you'll be asked for your name and optional skill level (2.0–8.0) in a small popup, then asked to confirm before you're added.

Tip: If you sign in with Google, your name, skill level, and preferences are saved automatically and pre-filled next time. Guests are asked fresh each time and nothing is saved.

The Queue

Player Badges

you — That's you in the list

user — A player using the app on their own device

partner — Your selected partner preference

Players added by someone else (rather than joining themselves) don't carry a badge — but whoever added them sees an edit (✎) icon to update their name, skill, or 1v1 preference.

  • + Add Player — Add players who don't have the app, or aren't joining themselves. You can edit or remove players you added.
  • Sit Out (⏸) — Sit out temporarily. You stay in the session but won't be assigned to courts. Anyone can sit out anyone.
  • Play (▶) — Rejoin the queue after sitting out.
  • Remove (×) — Remove yourself (leaves the session) or players you added. Admins can remove anyone.

Court Assignments

Open Play (default)

The app automatically assigns players to courts — doubles (2v2) first, then singles (1v1) if courts remain and players opted in.

  1. Set "Courts available now" — how many courts to fill this press.
  2. Tap Assign Courts. Players are randomly paired and assigned.
  3. Tap ▶ Start on a court to start its timer.
  4. When a game ends, tap End, then Save (with or without a score) or Cancel to go back — those players return to the queue.
  5. Tap Assign Courts again to fill open courts with waiting players.
Match timer: Once started, a court shows a live elapsed timer with a Pause/Resume toggle. The final duration is saved with the match in History.
Rematch: When ending a match, check the "Rematch" box before saving to immediately start the same matchup again.
Scores are optional: Tap Save without entering a score to end the match and log it without a result — this works the same way in Open Play, Round Robin, and Tournament.

Match Options

  • Match by skill (on by default) — Pairs players with similar skill levels.
  • Minimize repeats (on by default) — Avoids matching players who've already played each other.

Partner Preferences

In the Settings tab under Your Partner(s):

  • Tap + Add Partner and select a player.
  • When both you and a partner are in the queue, you'll be placed on the same side in doubles.
  • If a partner is on a court or sitting out, you'll be assigned normally with other players.
  • You can add multiple partners — the app will pair you with whichever one is available.

Partner preferences aren't available in offline sessions (Quick Play or Offline Session) — there's no Settings tab in those.

Share Link

Every online session has a Share Link in the Settings tab. Copy it and send to friends to invite them to your session. When someone opens a share link:

  • For private sessions, they'll be asked for the passcode first.
  • They can then sign in with Google or join as a guest, enter their info, and they're in.

Share links aren't available for offline sessions — those never leave your device.

Private Sessions

For games at home, tournaments, or groups that want their own space:

  1. Sign in with Google on the landing page (required to create).
  2. From the Join a Session screen, tap + Create New under Private Session.
  3. Enter a session name and a 4-character passcode (or tap Random to generate one).
  4. Choose Open Play, Round Robin, or Tournament from the session type dropdown.
  5. Tap Create & Join, confirm, then enter the starting player count and names — you're placed straight into the new session with your roster ready.
  6. Share the link with friends — they'll need the passcode to join. The session creator and super admins skip the passcode.
Passcode: Admins can view the session passcode in the Settings tab (click "Show" to reveal it). Share it with players who need to join.

Round Robin Mode

Round Robin is available for private (and offline) sessions and creates a structured tournament schedule:

  1. Add all players to the session.
  2. Tap Lock Players to Generate Schedule in the assign section.
  3. Set the number of courts and tap Generate Schedule.
  4. Round 1 is assigned to courts. All upcoming rounds are shown below.
  5. When all Round 1 games finish, tap Start Next Round to advance.

Admins can switch between Open Play, Round Robin, and Tournament in the Settings tab, and can unlock players to modify the roster. With an odd number of players, someone sits out each round; leftover unpaired players may get a singles match instead of doubles.

Tournament Mode

Tournament mode creates a single-elimination bracket for private (and offline) sessions:

  1. Add all players to the session.
  2. Tap Lock Players to Generate Bracket in the assign section.
  3. Set the number of courts and tap Generate Bracket.
  4. The bracket is displayed with all rounds. First-round matches are assigned to courts.
  5. When a match ends, enter scores — ties are not allowed in tournament matches. The winner automatically advances in the bracket. (Saving without a score just clears the court without recording a winner, so use it sparingly here.)
  6. When all matches in a round are complete, tap to start the next round (Semifinals, Final, etc.).
  7. If there are more matches than courts, remaining matches can be started as courts free up.
Seeding: Players are seeded by skill level (if set) so top seeds face lower seeds in early rounds. If fewer players than a power of 2, top seeds receive first-round byes. Tap 🖶 Save as PDF above the bracket to export it.

Match History

Session History

The History tab shows all completed matches in the current session with teams, scores, and match duration. Use the search bar to filter by player name, or tap My Matches to see only games you played in. Session history persists until an admin clears it.

Personal Match History

If you're signed in with Google, your matches are also saved to your personal match history. This history stays even if an admin clears the session history or deletes the session. Access it from the My Match History button in your profile card on the landing page. You can search by player name and swipe left on any match to delete it. Personal match history isn't available for offline or guest sessions.

Match Time Limit

Matches automatically expire after 100 minutes. Expired matches are saved to history with a score of "N/A" and players are returned to the queue.

Sharing & Multi-Device

  • All online session data syncs in real-time across everyone's devices. Offline sessions (Quick Play or Offline Session) stay on the one device that started them.
  • Court assignments, scores, and queue changes appear instantly for all users in an online session.
  • If you leave a session, your court assignments stay active for others to manage.
  • Share the URL or use the Share Link in Settings to invite others — public sessions don't need a code, private ones do.
  • Session data persists until an admin resets or deletes it — it won't disappear on its own.

Admin Features

There are two types of admins:

  • Session Admin — The person who created a session. Automatically gets admin for that session. Other signed-in users can be promoted to session admin from Settings.
  • Super Admin — Can manage any session (public or private), see all private sessions, create public locations, delete any session, and add/remove other super admins. Super admins also get an "All Private Sessions" and "All Users" panel above the join screen.

Managing Session Admins

In the Settings tab, admins can manage who else has admin access via the Session Admins card:

  • Tap + Add Admin to see a list of signed-in users in the session.
  • Select a user and confirm to grant them admin access.
  • Remove an admin by tapping "remove" next to their name.

Managing Super Admins

Existing super admins see a Super Admin Management panel on the landing screen (above the join options) where they can add or remove other super admins by email.

Admin Actions

Admins can:

  • Reset Everything — Clears all data. All users get kicked back to the join screen.
  • Clear History — Wipes match history and sit-out counts, keeps players and courts.
  • Delete Session — Permanently removes a session (with double confirmation). Available for private sessions and for super admins on public sessions.
  • Remove any player from the queue.
  • Change session type — Switch between Open Play, Round Robin, and Tournament (private sessions).
  • View the Admin History log of all admin actions.
  • View and share the session passcode from Settings.
Super Admins can join any session as "Admin only" (observe and manage without being added to the player queue) or as a regular player.
